How Claude fits into your day‑trading workflow

Claude is best used as a **teacher and explainer**, not as a signal generator. You bring the data: screenshots, indicator values, your plan. Claude helps you understand what your setup is showing.

Claude can help you:
  • Clarify what each indicator measures and why it matters.
  • Describe how your EMAs, VWAP, RSI, and volume interact.
  • Outline checklists for entries, risk, and exits (in general terms).
  • Review your journaled trades and highlight patterns.
Claude will not:
  • Tell you what to buy, sell, or short.
  • Predict which stock will “blow up” next.
  • Guarantee profits or reduce risk to zero.
  • Replace your own risk management or judgment.

Core indicator setup for intraday charts

This is a **common educational layout** many day traders study. It’s not a recommendation—just a structured way to see trend, value, momentum, and participation at a glance.

  • Timeframe: 5‑minute candles (with 1‑minute and 15‑minute as context).
  • Trend: EMA 9 & EMA 21 on price.
  • Value: Session VWAP on price.
  • Momentum: RSI 14 under the chart.
  • Participation: Volume bars with average volume line.

Use Claude to walk through this setup on any ticker you’re studying. Ask it to explain what each indicator is doing on the current candle, and how that compares to your rules.

Day‑trading routine using this setup

Pre‑market
  • Scan for gappers and news catalysts.
  • Mark pre‑market high/low and key levels.
  • Ask Claude to summarize the catalyst and risks.
At the open
  • Watch how price behaves around VWAP.
  • Note whether EMA 9/21 align with your bias.
  • Use Claude to describe the first 3–5 candles objectively.
Mid‑session
  • Avoid over‑trading chop; let EMAs and VWAP show structure.
  • Use RSI and volume to filter weak moves.
  • Ask Claude to review screenshots of good vs. bad trades.
End of day
  • Export screenshots of your charts and trades.
  • Journal your decisions and emotions.
  • Have Claude highlight patterns in your journal over time.
